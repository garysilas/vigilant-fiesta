# Writing Style Guide

## Tone Definition

**Serious**
- Treat the subject matter with gravity and respect
- Avoid flippancy, sarcasm, or dismissive language
- Convey that the topic matters and has real stakes

**Sharp**
- Use precise, incisive language
- Cut through noise with clarity and directness
- Every word should earn its place; eliminate fluff
- Challenge assumptions without being aggressive

**Culturally Aware**
- Acknowledge social, historical, and cultural context
- Recognize how different communities experience the same issue
- Avoid blind spots that come from narrow perspective
- Ground commentary in lived realities, not abstractions

---

## Sentence Style

**Controlled Pacing**
- Match sentence rhythm to emotional moment
- Use shorter sentences for impact, longer ones for complexity
- Build and release tension through cadence
- Avoid monotonous sentence length

**Varied Sentence Length**
- Alternate between punchy and flowing constructions
- One-sentence paragraphs are acceptable for emphasis
- Complex ideas may require compound structures
- Rhythm should feel intentional, not accidental

**Deliberate Pauses**
- Use punctuation to create breathing room (em-dashes, ellipses, periods)
- Breaks in flow signal shifts in thought or emotional weight
- Pauses build anticipation before delivering key points
- Silence in text becomes emphasis when spoken

---

## Hook Patterns

**Contradiction**
- Open with a statement that seems impossible or opposed to common sense
- Example: "The most successful people are the ones who failed the most."
- Forces the listener to lean in to resolve the tension

**Uncomfortable Truth**
- State something people know but rarely say aloud
- Example: "Most productivity advice is written by people who don't have real jobs."
- Creates immediate identification and tension

**Reframing Common Belief**
- Take a widely accepted idea and flip the perspective
- Example: "We don't have a shortage of time—we have a surplus of distractions we refuse to give up."
- Makes familiar feel fresh and debatable

---

## Narrative Principles

**Tension → Breakdown → Escalation → Message**

1. **Tension**
   - Establish what's at stake
   - Introduce the conflict or problem
   - Create emotional investment in the outcome

2. **Breakdown**
   - Examine the mechanics of the problem
   - Reveal hidden dynamics or contradictions
   - Challenge surface-level understanding

3. **Escalation**
   - Heighten the stakes or broaden the scope
   - Connect to larger patterns or consequences
   - Build toward the inevitable conclusion

4. **Message**
   - Land the core insight clearly
   - Leave the listener with something to carry
   - Resist the urge to summarize—end with impact

---

## Banned Patterns

**Generic Filler Phrases**
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The reality is..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "In today's world..."
- "Needless to say..."

**Vague Statements**
- Claims without specific grounding
- Aspirational language without concrete examples
- "Some people say..." without attribution
- Broad generalizations that dodge accountability

**Unsupported Claims**
- Statistics without sources
- Assertions presented as facts without evidence
- Predictions without reasoning
- Moral judgments without explained criteria
